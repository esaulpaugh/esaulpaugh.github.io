III

A horizontal move. A room with a view. The great-will of the Service had seen fit to transfer Duval directly opposite, to the other side of the corridor. An improvement. The window faced southeast. He could see the Mediterranean. Excellent: a sea view—universally desired. No more need for artificial light. The wooden desk: the same. To call this a lateral move was an exercise in over-modesty.

More expensive, and not without reason: you can't cheat the market.

He leafed through the latest raw files on shipments bound for the Bloc. Everything was up to date.

Accurate? Who can say.

In addition to his usual duties on export restrictions, he was now not only authorized but expected to actively investigate the theft of artworks. Call it a promotion. The sea glittered through the window, indifferent to Duval’s new good fortune.
